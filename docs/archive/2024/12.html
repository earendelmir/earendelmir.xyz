Overlay scrollbars on Chrome based browsers

22 Feb 2024  ·  1 min  ·  #web

earendelmir (Photo)

I've recently installed Brave Browser on my computer and tried using it for a couple of days. I quit after 5 minutes, and switched back to Firefox. I won't comment on the experience nor give opinions on Brave, it wouldn't be fair, but I must address one big turn-off that all Chrome-based browsers share: the scrollbar.

You have no idea how much I despise Chrome's scrollbar, and how lucky I feel to be using Firefox!
You might call me mad, or think I'm way to invested in this, but you can't tell me I'm wrong. Chrome's scrollbar:

  • is ugly
  • has no dark theme
  • causes content shift

Come on... it has no qualities.

Thankfully, there's a cure for this senseless design: overlay scrollbars.
They're slim, modern and adaptable; most importantly, they do not move your content left to right every time you switch to a new page.

If you use a Chrome based browser, do yourself a favour and enable this feature. Just paste this URL in the address bar and toggle the option:

chrome://flags/#overlay-scrollbars

No need to thank me.
